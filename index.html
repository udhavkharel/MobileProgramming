Countries and Capitals

Nepal- Kathmandu

Nepal is a landlocked Himalayan nation known for its breathtaking mountains, including Mount Everest. It is culturally rich with Hindu and Buddhist heritage, temples, and festivals. Tourism and agriculture form major parts of its economy. Kathmandu, the capital, is famous for ancient temples, UNESCO sites, and vibrant traditional culture.

India- New Delhi

India is one of the world’s largest democracies, known for its diverse religions, languages, and traditions. It has a rapidly growing economy, especially in IT, space research, and manufacturing. New Delhi is the political and administrative center, rich in history, monuments, and modern government institutions.

Japan- Tokyo

Japan is an advanced island nation famous for high technology, robotics, automobiles, and innovation. It blends ancient traditions like samurai culture and shrines with futuristic cities. Tokyo, the capital, is a global financial hub known for its skyscrapers, transportation systems, food culture, and clean urban lifestyle.

Australia- Canberra

Australia is known for its vast landscapes, wildlife, beaches, and stable economy. It is rich in natural resources and has a high standard of living, strong education, and healthcare systems. Canberra, the capital, is a planned city surrounded by forests and lakes, housing national institutions like Parliament House and museums.

France- paris

France is a European nation known for art, fashion, cuisine, and history. It is a global cultural leader and a major economy with strong industries like tourism, luxury goods, aerospace, and agriculture. Paris, the capital, is called the “City of Light,” famous for the Eiffel Tower, Louvre Museum, and romantic atmosphere.

United States- Washington, DC

The United States is one of the world’s most influential countries, leading in technology, military power, innovation, and entertainment. It has a diverse population and strong economy. Washington, D.C., the capital, is home to the White House, Capitol Hill, and world-famous museums and monuments.

China- Beijing

China is the world’s most populous nation and a global manufacturing and technology powerhouse. It has rapid economic growth, major global trade influence, and a deep ancient history. Beijing, the capital, is known for historic sites like the Forbidden City and Great Wall, as well as modern business and tech sectors.

Brazil- Brasilia

Brazil is the largest country in South America, famous for the Amazon rainforest, Carnival festival, and football culture. It has a resource-rich economy and diverse ecosystems. Brasília, the capital, is a planned city known for its modern architecture and government buildings.
